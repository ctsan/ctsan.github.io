Hierarchies - Logarithmic counting for humans

2016-01-02
Life Math
389 words 2 mins read

Contents

Assumption: Rearrangement of objects for counting is possible.

People are capable of processing and understanding a very specific range of patterns with ease. Likely a subset of those relevant to our past survivability. For instance we are unable to enumerate instantly small number of objects - for today’s requirements - and therefore we have to reduce the problem into maintanable chunks, appropriate for our capabilities. A way to reduce the problem is building hierarchies. But let’s examine an example

It is very clear that when the number increases the task of enumeration from a human becomes prone to errors (and also dull). Then a simple idea is applied: break them into groups! That’s essentially a one-level hierarchy and it won’t get us far. After using this approach for a while, the same problem will appear again. Thankfully the structure of the new problem is the same as the initial. That’s a good indication that recursion should be applied. The choice of number of objects per level is for discussion and is considered in the next paragraph.

Since we are going to confine ourselves in practical 2 dimensional media, We can end up in the following spatial arrangement for multilayer hiearchies.

In the depicted case we have a hierarchy of 5 - 5 - 4 - 5 objects per level. In each row (level 2) we have 25 objects. In each block (level-2) we have 100 objects, in each row of blocks we have 500 objects (level-3).

Notice that spatially, that arrangement created a meta row in level-3. In level-4 we would have a block of blocks. Thus we have recursion in the arrangement too.

Of course Hierarchy comes at a cost. In the previous arrangements we have a spatial cost. For instance the space in the initial linear arrangement was utilized in a more efficient manner.

Number of Objects per level

Each level of counting in our example has 4 or 5 objects. This choice is not random. We are known to be able to count 1-5 objects nearly instantly, let’s say, in $\mathcal O(1)$. After that we need to enumerate, using linear counting ‘algorithm’ of $\mathcal O(n)$.

If we only use 5 meta-items per level, we have a capacity of $5^n$ items with $n$ levels. To evaluate the count we need about $n$ operations too. That’s logarithmic counting.
