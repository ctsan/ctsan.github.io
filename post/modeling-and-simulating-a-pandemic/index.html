Modelling and Simulating a Pandemic

In this blog post I attempt to model the spread and effects of a disease. This is a very timely topic with the emergence of COVID-19. Being able to simulate the disease is invaluable, particularly when combined with the simulation of mitigation approaches.

This model is household-based, i.e. we have families living in the same building. Every person has a constant network (ego-network) of people they can potentially interact with. For example you can think of this network as all the people you have interacted with in the last 100 days (or some other time frame long enough so that the outcome of the disease have been determined). Denote the network of citizen $u$ as $N_u$.

After fixing $N_u$, we have a dynamic network of temporary interactions. For example the daily interactions of a person. Denote this as $N_u(t) = \{ v : v \in N_u, v \text{ interacted with u at time t} \}$. To decide if $u,v$ interacted with each other during, say, a day we consider a few parameters: If any of the two are sick with symptoms - the severity of the symptoms, the social distancing parameter of the individual which is based on a biased random walk. The random walk can be adjusted depending on the governmental guidelines, i.e. whether the government is urging people to go out or stay at home (this is controlled by pressing “O” or “I” respectively).

The user can click at two points succesively in order to place a quarantine (rectangular in shape). People within the quarantine can not interact with people outside. Possibly in a future version it will be explored if an automatic quarantine scheme can lead to good control of the spread - relatively to some penalty term that prevents the overuse of bigger quarantines.
