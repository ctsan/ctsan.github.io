It was announced in Greek media that Turkey achieved a new maximum range with modern missiles in its disposal.

Immediately the question that I was curious about was, what targets in Greece can be hit by Turkey now?

Before I begin, I want to make clear that this experiment was created for fun and that I am not that pessimistic about the relations of the two countries.

Problem

Hit Area

if \(\mathcal{T}\) the set of points that are in Turkey. \(\mathcal{G}\) the set of points in Greece. The range of the missile \(R\), then we need to find:

$$\mathcal {Hit} = \{ x | y \in \mathcal{T}, x \in \mathcal G: dist(x,y) \leq R \}$$

Accessibility of Targets

It might be of interest how easily a target can be hit , that is, how many points in Turkey’s territory can be used to hit that target.

This can be described by the measure of the set of the points that can reach the target, non-normalized:

$$\mathcal{Acc}(x) =||\{ y | y \in \mathcal{T}, dist(x,y) \leq R \}||$$

Implementation

There are many ways to implement this. With sophisticated solutions, you can find a very precise image of the targets that can be hit.

For example, one could adopt computational geometry methods, and find nearly exact results with intersections of polygonal regions.

To make things very simple, my implementation was very direct: taking random points in Turkey’s territory using rejection. For any given random point \(A\) in Turkey, take random points \( \{x_i\} \) within the range of the missile: \( x_i \in \{ x | d(x,A) \leq range\}\). Keep the points that fall in Greek land \( \{x_i\} \cap \mathcal G \).

This is not an efficient way but it’s working for my purposes. Due to the fractal like topology of Greek and Turkey border, the problem becomes more demanding, but this immediate approach that works on a per group of pixels base doesn’t get affected by that.

Smarter methods could take advantage of the topology, or adopt better ways to sample the space. A better alternative would be to use markov chain monte carlo (MCMC) to sample the space.
