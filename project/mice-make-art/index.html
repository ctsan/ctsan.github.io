Concept

The concept is the following: Having N mice, deploy them randomly in a rectangular area. Each mouse targets exactly one other mouse. Every mouse is targeted by one mouse, forming a circle of references-targets.

Phenomena

  • Each mouse \(A\) is confined in the rectangular area: since it targets a mouse \(B\) that is within the rectangular area, it moves in this direction. Mouse \(B\) itself targets a mouse \(C\) which for the same reason is in the rectangular area. Since every moment every mouse targets a point within the rectangular area, they are restricted in this area.

  • Each mouse gets very close to its target mouse. Since the rectangular area is finite, a mouse has to take a turn at some point in order to be confined in the rectangular area. But since it is taking a turn, during this turn, the pursuing mouse reduces the distance from its target (since they have equal magnitude of velocity). This happens until a lower-bound distance is reached.

  • The mice reach a steady state circulating on a path. In this phase each mouse is near the lower-bound distance from its target.

  • Usually a loop of mice emerges. The more the mice deployed, the bigger the length of the curve. The lower-bounds of distances define the length of that loop.
